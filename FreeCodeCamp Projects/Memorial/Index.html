Dr. Ozi Bravo

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a time line of Dr. Ozi's life:

  • 1974 - Born in Soviet Russia
  • 1990 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Konya, thanks
    to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1995 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian
    Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed
    them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1997 - Finishes university and takes a job in the Turkish Forestry Service
  • 1998 - Marries wife of 30 years Margret Gibson. Gets laid off due to budget
    cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, he returns to school study under
    Stakman, who teaches him about breeding pest-resistent plants.
  • 1999 - Tries to enroll in the military after the Edirne attack,
    but is rejected. Instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue,
    DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 2000 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 2002 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnant wife,
    and flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program. Over the next 16 years, his team breeds
    6,000 different strains of disease resistent wheat - including different varieties for each major climate on Earth.
